Catty 3D is a terminal that lives in 3D space. Your shell renders as a textured surface inside a RealityKit scene, with a tiny cat watching the cursor and a rat orbiting nearby — your familiar prompt, suddenly inhabiting a small universe of its own.

Open as many panes as you like. Spawn them in any cardinal direction from any existing terminal — left, right, up, down. The layout grows organically into an infinite grid, each pane with its own shell, its own cursor cat, its own surface mode.

What's inside:

• Local shell sessions in any folder you choose
• SSH sessions to remote hosts (Citadel-backed)
• Four surface modes per pane: flat plane, cinemascope curve, Möbius strip, centre-bulge warp
• Selection glow that follows the active pane as you orbit the camera
• Multi-pane spawning on an expanding grid — no fixed limit
• Persistence across launches: relaunch and your panes are right where you left them
• Comical zoom range — pull the camera back until the whole cluster is a postage stamp against the starfield
• Open source under the MIT license

Catty 3D is built on top of SwiftTerm and Citadel, with 3D assets gratefully borrowed from Orhun's Ratty (the rat) and bean's Maxwell-the-cat USDZ on Sketchfab (the cat). Full attribution lives inside the app and on GitHub.

This is the first public release. Feedback and contributions welcome at github.com/mochiexists/catty.
